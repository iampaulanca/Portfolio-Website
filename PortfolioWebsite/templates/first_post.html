Building projects is the best way to learn everything

Posted by Paul Ancajima on July 15, 2019
{% endblock header %} {% block content %}

The title says it all, software employers nowadays need to programmers to have enough knowledge to hit the floor running. That's fine but, where does that put new grads that just began their journey? I recently graduated from San Francisco State University with a Bachelors in Computer Science. You would think there would be jobs lined up out the door for someone with a CS degree in San Francisco, wrong. Competition is fierce and people from around the world are coming to the bay area for tech jobs and they are typically coming with experience. I wouldn't recommend coming to the most expensive place to live in the U.S. without a game plan.

Learning Never Stops

Back to my current situation. After a month of applying to 40 different job postings I landed a whopping 2 interviews. The first interview went very well typical coding interview with a whiteboard test but, the second interview is the reason I'm building this site. My university didn't have many courses on web development. The emphasis was so small, it caused my software engineering professor to raise hell and had additional courses created as prerequisites. That was one of my favorite classes by the way. Within a summer session, I had to learn Ruby, Ruby on Rails, MySQL, and GitHub (with a team) but, I also learned a lot about myself. I've come to realize that I grow the most under pressure and become extremely resourceful. Diving in head first and coming out successful has absolutely boosted my confidence and determination.

Since graduating I decided to start setting daily goals whether it's to complete a couple of LeetCode challenges, work on my blog, or anything else. Today's goal is to remove the filler text that came with this BootStrap template and write something significant. If you've been clicking around you can tell most of my site is not ready for launch but hey, this is a learning experience. My blog site is slowly progressing and if for some reason you're back even after witnessing the chaotic mess that I call my blog site, from the bottom of my heart I just want to thank you. Hopefully, I'll have a working comment section soon so, I can read any kind of feedback.

Life is about how much you can take and keep fighting, how much you can suffer and keep moving forward.
- Anderson Silva
